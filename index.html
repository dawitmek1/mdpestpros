Bed Bugs

Small reddish-brown insects that feed on blood while you sleep. They hide in mattress seams, furniture cracks, and baseboards — and spread room to room rapidly.

A single female lays up to 500 eggs in her lifetime.
They survive up to 1 full year without feeding.
Store-bought sprays do not kill eggs — professional treatment required.

Seeing "Just a Few" Pests?

If you're seeing even a few pests, there's already a much bigger problem you can't see. Every day you wait, the infestation — and the cost — grows.

Droppings near baseboards or cabinets

Almost always signals a hidden rodent or roach infestation inside walls.

Red bites or welts when you wake up

Classic bedbug sign. They spread room-to-room within days if not treated.

Hollow-sounding wood or mud tubes

Termites silently destroy structural wood for years before any damage shows.
